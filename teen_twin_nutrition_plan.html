Middle school nutrition plan for 14-year-old twin boys — Vancouver BC

Nutritionist's note: True low-carb is not appropriate for growing teen athletes — pubescent bodies and active muscles run on carbohydrates. This plan is protein-forward with quality carbs and healthy fats: filling, growth-supporting, and sports-ready. At 50 kg / 5'4", target intake is ~2,400–2,800 cal/day; these boys are lean for their height and need to eat well.
Monday

Chicken & Cheese Quesadilla Stack

Main
2 large flour tortillas, Sunday-grilled chicken strips, shredded marble cheese, salsa on the side
Sides
String cheese + small handful of almonds + apple slices
Night-before prep
Slice batch-cooked chicken, layer with cheese between two tortillas, wrap in foil. Ready in 4 min.
School microwave
Unwrap foil, place on paper towel, 60–90 sec. Folds like a pizza slice — totally normal teen food.
~48g protein ~670 cal 4 min night-before microwave ✓
Tuesday

Meatball Sub with Melted Mozzarella

Main
Sub roll, 5–6 Kirkland beef meatballs, marinara sauce, shredded mozzarella
Sides
Baby carrots + hummus tub + clementine
Night-before prep
Portion meatballs from Sunday batch into a container. Pack sauce separately so bread stays dry.
School microwave
Microwave meatballs + sauce 90 sec, pour over sub roll, top with mozzarella. Let sit 20 sec to melt.
~44g protein ~690 cal 3 min night-before microwave ✓
Wednesday

Turkey & Cheese Pinwheel Wraps

Main
Large tortilla, Kirkland sliced turkey breast, marble cheese, Dijon mustard, romaine lettuce
Sides
Kirkland Greek yogurt (full-fat) + granola bar + grape tomatoes
Night-before prep
Roll tight, wrap in cling film or beeswax wrap, refrigerate. Self-pack in 3 min. Boys can slice at school.
No microwave needed
Served cold — looks just like store-bought sushi rolls when sliced into pinwheels. Cool factor is high.
~44g protein ~630 cal 3 min night-before
Thursday

Pepperoni Pizza Wrap — the crowd pleaser

Main
Flour tortilla, jarred pizza sauce, shredded mozzarella, Kirkland pepperoni, Italian herbs
Sides
String cheese + trail mix + apple
Night-before prep
Spread sauce, scatter cheese and pepperoni, fold into quarters, wrap in foil. 4 min total.
School microwave
45–60 sec. Cheese melts perfectly. It's a personal pizza — every kid at the table will want one.
~38g protein ~700 cal 4 min night-before microwave ✓
Friday

Teriyaki Chicken Rice Box

Main
Jasmine or brown rice, Sunday-grilled chicken, Kirkland teriyaki sauce, frozen edamame (thawed)
Sides
Banana + protein bar (their Friday "treat" — earns it after a full week)
Night-before prep
Layer rice → chicken → sauce → edamame in leak-proof container. 5 min. Looks like takeout.
School microwave
2 min, stir halfway. The most popular lunch at the table — the good kind of attention.
~52g protein ~720 cal 5 min night-before microwave ✓
Sunday Batch Cook (35–45 min, once a week): Grill 1.5 kg chicken breast on a pan or BBQ → portion for Mon & Fri. Cook a large pot of jasmine rice → Friday boxes. Reheat and portion meatballs → Tuesday container. Pack Monday–Wednesday boxes tonight. Thursday and Friday boxes go in fridge Wednesday night. Zero morning effort all week.
Active 14-year-old boys burn 2,400–2,800 cal/day. Without strategic snacking, energy crashes mid-afternoon and sports performance tanks. The after-school window (4–6 PM) is particularly important for muscle recovery during puberty.
10:00–10:30 AM
Recess / break
Protein bar + piece of fruit
Kirkland protein bar or similar (aim for 15g+ protein, not a candy bar). Apple, banana, or clementine. Fast to eat, looks completely normal.
12:30–1:00 PM
Lunch block
Weekly lunch box + sides (see Weekly Plan tab)
630–720 cal, 38–52g protein depending on the day. This is the anchor meal. Don't skip sides — the string cheese and nuts matter.
4:00–4:30 PM
After school / pre-practice
Trail mix + string cheese + banana
The banana provides fast glucose to fuel the first 20 min of practice. Trail mix sustains energy through drills. String cheese keeps muscle protein topped up. Pack this in the school bag the night before.
6:00–7:00 PM
Post-practice recovery
Greek yogurt + granola + almonds
The 30–60 min post-exercise window is the most important for muscle repair in pubescent athletes. Full-fat Greek yogurt (casein + whey) is ideal. This also prevents them from raiding everything before dinner.
The "snack station" — 60 second self-packing
Keep a basket on the kitchen counter stocked with pre-portioned bags of trail mix, almonds, and granola bars. String cheese lives on the fridge door shelf. Boys grab what they need and pack their own bags the night before, independently. This builds their own nutrition habits and removes your morning load entirely.
Hydration reminder
Teen athletes commonly mistake thirst for hunger. A 1L water bottle (filled the night before) should go in every bag. Optional: a small chocolate milk box in the lunch bag covers post-lunch protein and electrolytes in one hit — and it's something all teens drink without a second thought.
All options below pack in under 3 minutes — some in 30 seconds. Keep a dedicated "backup shelf" in the pantry always stocked. These are not lesser options; they're smart fallbacks that still meet their nutritional needs.
1
Deli rolls — 30 seconds easiest possible
Roll 4–5 slices of Kirkland turkey or ham around a piece of marble cheese. Drop in a container with a handful of crackers and an apple. No prep. No mess. Protein-forward and filling.
~35g protein~500 cal
2
PB & jam on whole wheat — 2 minutes classic standby
Two thick slices, generous Kirkland peanut butter, jam. Add string cheese and a banana. Universally normal at any school table. Calorie-dense and surprisingly protein-rich from the PB.
~22g protein~580 cal
3
Rotisserie chicken + crackers — 1 minute high protein
Pull apart last night's Costco rotisserie chicken into a container. Add a handful of crackers, cheese cubes, and baby carrots. Microwave the chicken at school. No prep needed — this is pure pantry-and-fridge assembly.
~45g protein~550 cal
4
Grab bag — 30 seconds zero cooking
Protein bar + string cheese + trail mix bag + apple. Everything comes from the snack station or fridge door. No thought, no prep, no dishes. Keep this as the absolute floor option — it still provides ~30g protein and sustained energy.
~30g protein~520 cal
5
Cold quesadilla — 3 minutes morning microwave at home
Tortilla + shredded cheese, 90 sec in home microwave, cool, fold, pack cold. It's genuinely good at room temperature — similar to a cold cheese slice. Add salsa cup, string cheese, and apple.
~28g protein~580 cal
6
Tuna pouch + crackers + cheese — 1 minute pantry-only
Kirkland tuna pouches require no can opener and no draining. One pouch + Triscuits or Ryvita + marble cheese cubes. Very high protein, omega-3 rich, and impressively fast. Add an apple to round it out.
~38g protein~480 cal
